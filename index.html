Erin Scot

Who I Am

A little more than two years ago, I decided to get into the technology realm. I had spent the previous decade working in various capacities in Hollywood. After eight years in L.A. I had moved to the Pacific Northwest, where I made my living transcribing interviews for mind-numbing reality television shows. That was, surprisingly, not very fulfilling. I was 34 and thought it might be too late to start a new career, but I went back to school to get a second bachelor's degree, this time in computer information technology. In the meantime, I took classes and applied for jobs in the industry.

What I Do Now

Without knowing a thing about web performance, I applied to a job as "consulting support" in web performance analysis. Luckily, I have a way with words, and my cover letter and subsequent interviews got me in the door. Long story short, I still haven't finished that second degree, but I've spent the last 26 months working in web performance for Dynatrace (formerly Keynote). So I look at waterfalls all day to understand how web page design affects performance.

What I Want to Do

I'm moving up at Dynatrace, with a recent promotion to "Performance Guardian." That means the clients I've been supporting (a major domestic car company and an international cell phone carrier) are about to be disappointed to learn I'm moving on. I'll soon have my own (smaller) clients. But I'm learning how to develop websites myself because I enjoy this time of work. So eventually I'd like to be able to call myself a web designer/developer. My experience in knowing how to put together a website for maximum performance should come in handy as I learn... how to put together a website. In the meantime, monitoring and analyzing others' sites is what pays the bills.
